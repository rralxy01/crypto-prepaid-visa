## What "Prepaid Visa with Crypto" Actually Means

A prepaid Visa that runs on crypto is not magic. It is a normal Visa card — same network, same merchant acceptance, same 3DS prompts you see on any online checkout — except the balance behind it is loaded from cryptocurrency instead of a bank account.

When you tap or check out, the card processor converts the required amount of crypto into fiat at that moment and settles with the merchant in fiat. You never have to manually swap USDT to USD in a separate app, wait for a bank transfer, or explain to your bank why a Hong Kong-issued card is paying for a French SaaS subscription.

Within this category there are three practical shapes:

- **Virtual cards** — issued in minutes, live in your phone, bind to Apple Pay / Google Pay / PayPal, perfect for subscriptions and online purchases.
- **Physical cards** — plastic, ship to your door, work at POS terminals and ATMs.
- **Hybrid custody models** — some keep your crypto in their custodial balance until you spend (PokePay, RedotPay, Bybit); others let you keep keys until the moment of payment (MetaMask Card, Cypher, Oobit).

For most people searching "prepaid Visa with crypto," the journey starts with a **virtual card** because it is cheap, fast, and reversible. Physical cards come later, once you trust the platform.
